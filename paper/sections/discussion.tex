\label{sec:discussion}

The discussion will interpret the final experimental results from a deployment
perspective. The central point is that railway-domain LLM selection under a
single-GPU constraint is a multi-objective decision rather than a leaderboard
ranking.

The final paper should discuss whether larger models consistently outperform
smaller models, whether int4 inference provides an acceptable quality-efficiency
tradeoff, and whether QLoRA changes the model recommendation. If a smaller
model adapts strongly to railway data, it may be more useful in practice than a
larger baseline model with higher latency.

Limitations should be stated explicitly. The study is limited to one GPU class,
one local railway-domain corpus, and open models that are available in the local
environment. It does not claim to introduce a new algorithm, and it should not
generalize beyond the evaluated hardware and dataset without additional
experiments.
