\label{sec:problem_setting}

The target application is local railway-domain question answering and bilingual
technical assistance. The intended users are railway engineering students,
laboratory researchers, and technical staff who need fast access to
domain-specific terminology, translation, and regulation-oriented information.
The model receives a short prompt and is expected to return a concise answer
without long unrelated explanations or unsafe operational recommendations.

The task space has three parts. The first is bilingual terminology lookup,
where a railway technical expression is mapped between Chinese and English. The
second is technical translation, where the model translates a short railway
operation or maintenance expression. The third is evidence-grounded regulatory
QA, where the answer should be recoverable from a provided source passage or
from a known regulatory clause. The first two tasks test domain vocabulary and
wording. The third tests whether the model can answer regulatory questions
without drifting away from evidence.

The deployment constraint is fixed: all inference and adaptation must be
feasible on one NVIDIA RTX 3090 with 24GB of VRAM. This excludes multi-GPU
serving, full-parameter fine-tuning for larger models, and large
hyperparameter searches. The resulting model-selection problem is therefore
multi-objective. A good candidate must fit in memory, respond with usable
latency, retain broad public benchmark quality, and support low-cost domain
adaptation.

The paper treats missing results as part of the current project state rather
than as hidden negative evidence. Existing exports are reported directly.
Unfinished runs are marked as placeholders in the tables and should be filled
by rerunning the corresponding experiment scripts under the same configuration.
