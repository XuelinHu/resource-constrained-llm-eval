Open large language models (LLMs) provide a promising route for railway-domain
question answering and bilingual technical assistance, but many deployment
settings cannot rely on multi-GPU infrastructure. This paper studies the
evaluation, quantization, and QLoRA-based domain adaptation of open LLMs under a
single RTX 3090 24GB constraint. We construct a railway-domain bilingual QA
dataset from traction power supply regulations and railway technical
terminology, and evaluate a locally deployable model pool covering Qwen, Qwen
2.5, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, Gemma, Phi, Yi, and Mistral families. The study is
positioned as an application-oriented and experimental evaluation rather than a
new model architecture. It asks which open models are practically usable under a
single-GPU budget, how low-bit inference changes the performance-efficiency
tradeoff, and whether QLoRA improves railway-domain performance without
exceeding the available hardware budget. The expected outcome is a reproducible
framework for resource-constrained railway LLM evaluation and adaptation,
together with empirical guidance for model selection in engineering education
and railway technical applications.
