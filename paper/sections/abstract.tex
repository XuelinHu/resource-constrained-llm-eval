Open large language models below 8B parameters are increasingly attractive for laboratories and vertical applications that cannot rely on multi-GPU infrastructure. However, many existing comparisons emphasize benchmark scores alone and do not adequately characterize the deployment tradeoff between task quality, memory footprint, latency, throughput, and adaptation cost under realistic single-GPU constraints. This paper studies seven open models that can be deployed on a single RTX 3090 24GB GPU, including Qwen3 models from 0.6B to 8B, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, and Gemma 3 4B. We evaluate these models on general knowledge, mathematical reasoning, code generation, Chinese evaluation, and a domain-specific benchmark, and we further apply QLoRA-based adaptation to representative models under the same hardware budget. The study is designed to answer three practical questions: which models provide the best performance-efficiency tradeoff, how much domain improvement QLoRA can deliver on consumer hardware, and whether reasoning-oriented small models justify their additional inference cost. The expected contribution is a reproducible evaluation and adaptation pipeline together with deployment-oriented model selection guidance for single-GPU environments.
