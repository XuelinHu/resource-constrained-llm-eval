Local open large language models (LLMs) are attractive for domain-specific
technical assistance, but many laboratories can only rely on a single
workstation GPU. This paper reorganizes a PeerJ-style application study of
resource-constrained LLM evaluation and adaptation for railway-domain question
answering on one NVIDIA RTX 3090 24GB GPU. The latest project plan defines a
ten-model local pool spanning Qwen, DeepSeek, Mistral, Phi, Yi, Gemma, and GLM
families; two domain datasets; public benchmark evaluation; low-bit inference
measurement; and QLoRA adaptation. Domain-QA contains 22,164/2,770/2,770
train/validation/test examples for bilingual railway terminology and
translation. Domain-RegQA contains 1,600/200/200 evidence-grounded regulatory
QA examples generated by rule-based extraction from source passages. Current
exports include int4 public and domain results for Qwen3-4B and Qwen3-8B,
int4 efficiency rows for four 4B--8B models, and Adapter-A QLoRA training or
evaluation artifacts for Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and Qwen3-8B. On the exported
int4 public benchmark rows, Qwen3-8B obtains the strongest average score
(0.539 across MMLU, C-Eval, GSM8K, and HumanEval), followed by Qwen3-4B
(0.486). On Domain-QA, Qwen3-8B reaches 0.317 character-level F1, while
Qwen3-4B reaches 0.294. Domain-RegQA remains difficult, with exact match at
zero for both exported baselines. The manuscript keeps unfinished ten-model,
mixed-adapter, and completion-only experiments as explicit placeholders. The
result is a coherent paper draft that separates completed measurements from
planned experiments and supports the next stage of full empirical completion.
