Open large language models (LLMs) are attractive for railway-domain bilingual
question answering, but many laboratories and educational deployments are
limited to a single workstation GPU. This paper presents an application-oriented
evaluation and adaptation study under a fixed NVIDIA RTX 3090 24GB constraint.
We build a railway-domain bilingual dataset from technical terminology and
traction power supply operation-and-maintenance material, and evaluate locally
cached open models from the Qwen, DeepSeek, Phi, Yi, and Mistral families. The
current result export contains completed baseline and efficiency measurements
for six models across bf16, int8, and int4 settings, plus an initial QLoRA
adapter evaluation for two models. On the completed public benchmark runs,
Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and Qwen3-8B obtain the strongest overall scores, while
Qwen3-0.6B and Qwen3-1.7B provide much lower memory footprints and faster bf16
throughput. Low-bit inference substantially reduces memory for several models,
but the measured int4 and int8 paths are not always faster than bf16 on the
tested software stack. The Domain QA and QLoRA exports currently contain only a
one-sample sanity check, so they are reported as pipeline-completeness evidence
rather than final domain-effectiveness claims. The study contributes a
reproducible single-GPU evaluation workflow, a railway bilingual QA dataset, a
curated literature matrix, and deployment-oriented guidance for continuing the
full domain evaluation.
