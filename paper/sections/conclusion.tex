\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper investigates open LLMs up to 8B parameters under a strict single-GPU constraint and studies how QLoRA changes their practical utility for domain-specific deployment. The central premise is that model selection on a consumer GPU is a joint optimization problem over quality, memory, latency, and adaptation cost rather than a simple benchmark ranking exercise.

The final version of the paper should conclude with three layers of findings: which model is the strongest baseline, which model provides the best overall deployment tradeoff, and which model responds most effectively to low-cost QLoRA adaptation. If these three answers differ, that difference is itself an important result because it shows that the best benchmark model is not always the best deployed model.

The main practical contribution is a reproducible experimental and writing pipeline that supports systematic evaluation, lightweight domain adaptation, and evidence-driven model selection on a single RTX 3090. This makes the study directly useful for laboratories and engineering teams that need publishable analysis and deployable results under realistic hardware constraints.
