\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper draft reorganizes a resource-constrained local LLM study for
railway-domain QA on a single RTX 3090 24GB GPU. It aligns the manuscript with
the latest work plan: a ten-model open LLM pool, two railway-domain datasets,
public benchmark evaluation, int4 efficiency measurement, and QLoRA adaptation
with planned data-composition ablations.

The completed exports show that Qwen3-8B is currently the strongest int4
public-benchmark baseline among the filled rows, while Qwen3-4B offers a lower
memory alternative. Domain-QA performance is modest and Domain-RegQA remains
difficult, confirming the need for domain-specific evaluation rather than
public benchmarks alone. QLoRA Adapter-A training is operational within the
24GB limit, but the current adaptation evidence indicates that additional
mixed-data and completion-only variants are needed before making final
deployment claims.

The manuscript is now structured so that unfinished experiments are explicit
placeholders instead of implicit gaps. The next version should replace those
placeholders with the final ten-model sweep, matched baseline-versus-adapter
domain evaluations, data-composition ablations, and qualitative error analysis.
