\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper studies resource-constrained railway-domain LLM evaluation and
adaptation under a single RTX 3090 24GB GPU budget. The work is positioned as
an application-oriented experimental study. Its contribution is the combination
of a railway bilingual QA dataset, a reproducible multi-model evaluation
pipeline, low-bit inference analysis, and QLoRA-based domain adaptation.

The final conclusion will summarize which model is most accurate, which model
is most efficient, and which model benefits most from QLoRA. These findings
will provide practical guidance for railway education and technical-support
applications that need locally deployable open LLMs without multi-GPU
infrastructure.
